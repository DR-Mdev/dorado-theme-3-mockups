How we operate

Market development, run like an engineering system.

Most agencies describe their method in adjectives. We can describe ours in database tables.

Go-to-market work fails quietly: messages go out, meetings happen, and a year later nobody can say what worked, what didn't, or why. We built our own operating system to make that impossible. Every hypothesis, every outreach, every reply, and every dead end is recorded, measured, and turned into a reusable asset — for us and for our clients.

The spine

One structure
for everything.

Underneath every engagement runs the same spine. The structured backbone lives in a database; the depth — research memos, message archives, account dossiers — lives in versioned files, linked to it. Nothing depends on anyone's memory. If a project pauses for three months, it resumes from the file, not from recollection.

Hypotheses, not hunches

Every thesis is written down
as a falsifiable claim.

Every market thesis we run is a formal hypothesis. Each one is tested through batches: fixed cohorts of real companies — a pilot batch first, then validation cohorts of hundreds — with response rates measured per company, not per anecdote.

A hypothesis, as written

“Companies of this profile, under this trigger, with this buyer — will respond.”

No batch is allowed to just fade out. Every one ends with an explicit call:

Exploratory pilots are never extrapolated into conclusions — that's a rule, not a habit.

No hand-typed metrics Reply rates, contact counts, and last-activity dates are derived from the event log itself. If a number is on our dashboard, there's a recorded event behind it.

Three motions

Not all outreach
is the same work.

Cold list work, warm follow-up, and strategic account pursuit are different jobs with different rhythms. Forcing them into one pipeline is how CRMs turn into graveyards. We run them as three explicit motions, and every company in the system is in exactly one:

List
The cold pool · high rotation

Fast, evidence-based sorting

High-rotation qualification of researched companies. The goal is not volume of messages — it's a fast, evidence-based sort: who is worth pursuing, who isn't, and why.

Follow-up
Person-level · warm

Warm interest never expires

The moment someone replies, the unit of work changes from company to person. Each warm contact gets a next action with a date — our queue won't let a lead be closed without one.

ABM
Account-level · strategic

Coverage, reviewed weekly

Some accounts justify pursuit before anyone replies. We map the key people and track coverage — who has been reached, who has responded, where the gaps are — against the actual contact log, not optimism.

The message library

Messages are measured
like product features.

Every message we send is archived verbatim with its metadata: what the hook was, which template it came from, whether it got a reply, and how effective it proved. Templates earn their place — a draft only becomes a reusable template after it has demonstrably worked, and the library records what failed, too.

Hooks built on the prospect's own situation earn same-day replies. Generic industry news earns silence. We know this because we measured it — and every future message benefits from it.

The handoff

The exit is designed in
from day one.

The end state of our work is not dependence on Dorado. When an account is qualified — real problem, real owner, real timeline — it is packaged through standard handoff templates and transferred into your own CRM.
