01 Validate Market research & entry validation / stage 01 of 03

Know before you enter.
Validate before you invest.

“Japan looks like the obvious next market. But every plan we have is built on assumptions we can't check from here.”

The situation this stage exists for

Overseas companies entering Japan fall into the same trap: strategies built on assumptions that don't survive contact with the market. We run structured research sprints that turn "we think Japan could work" into a prioritized, evidence-backed entry plan, including the uncomfortable findings that change it.

01: Decode

Decode why you win at home

Before asking whether your success can travel, we reverse-engineer where it comes from, and sort your advantages into what transfers to Japan and what is anchored in your home market.

02: Measure

Size the market, price the reality

Market sizing under multiple scenarios, competitor teardowns built from real user complaints, and benchmarks from real shelves and real menus, not industry averages. Where direct data doesn't exist, we build defensible proxy models from public statistics instead of stopping at "no data."

03: Check

Map the rules foreign entrants miss

Invoice systems, labeling and compliance standards, subsidy dynamics, payment culture: the local details that quietly decide whether a product is sellable, treated as strategy inputs, not footnotes.

04: Test

Test your hypothesis honestly

You usually arrive with a candidate plan. We score it against evidence, and when the data pushes back, we say so. Every research task exists to confirm or kill a specific assumption, nothing is researched "for completeness."

05: Arm

End with a plan you can execute

Findings flow into a sequenced entry strategy: the beachhead segment, the channel logic, vetted target lists, and sales materials in Japanese: assets you can use the week the engagement ends.

Deliverable A decision, not a report. The output is a go / no-go call with a beachhead, the reasoning behind each rejected alternative, and what to test next, documented so your board can audit it.

Engagement shape

30-day sprint→ Weekly reviews→ Go / no-go call→ Monthly validation

The first month is a fixed-scope research sprint with weekly checkpoints. If the call is "go," the same structure extends month-to-month into hypothesis validation (tastings, interviews, first outreach) without re-scoping from zero.

02 Sell Go-to-market operations / stage 02 of 03

Interest was never your problem.

“A full calendar of demos, free pilots, and partner talks, and no revenue.”

The situation this stage exists for

For most companies entering Japan, interest is easy, and none of it converts. The gap is everything after interest: who owns the problem inside the buyer, what budget pays for it, and what the finished, purchasable package even is. We run that commercial layer as an operating system, not a series of favors.

1

Diagnose the pipeline honestly

Every open opportunity audited and graded, then a deal-by-deal priority list: push, nurture, or stop investing entirely. Telling you what to stop doing is half the value.

2

Find where the demand actually is

Candidate segments researched in depth before anyone sells anything: does the problem exist, who owns it, what volume, can it pay?

3

Test ideas against evidence, including yours

When you propose a positioning, we score it, not applaud it. Clients pay us to be right, not agreeable.

4

Put numbers on it first

Market sizing, volume estimates per prospect, revenue models per scenario, built before outreach, so effort goes where the economics work.

5

Run the outreach and keep deals warm

We send the messages, track every reply, and never let warm interest expire. Every contact has a next action with a date: our own system won't let us close a lead without one.

6

Stand in the room

We sit in the live conversations, learn both your technology and the buyer's approval process, and turn every interaction into a documented, reusable asset.

Engagement shape

Lane→ Batch→ Call→ Repeat or graduate

This stage runs as scoped engagements: you buy defined deliverables, not hours. Each market question is tested through batches of real companies, and every batch ends with an explicit call: continue, adjust, or stop spending. The machinery behind it is on the How we operate page; the meters are on Pricing.

03 Operate Customer success representation / stage 03 of 03

Your customer success team in Japan.

“The contract is signed. The customer speaks Japanese, the site is in Tokyo, and our whole team is nine time zones away.”

The situation this stage exists for

Overseas technology companies often win their first Japanese customer before they have any presence in Japan. A translation agency can't close that gap: the customer needs someone accountable for the relationship, not just the words. We act as your embedded customer success function on the ground.

01: Represent

Own the relationship locally

We attend the meetings (online and on-site, internal and customer-facing), provide interpretation, write concise bilingual summaries, and chase the action items so nothing stalls between time zones.

02: Translate

Keep deeply technical content precise

Audit reports, engineering documentation, system point lists: translated into natural, technically accurate Japanese, plus executive summaries your customer's leadership can act on.

03: Coordinate

Handle the ground game

Site visits, data collection, scheduling with local contractors, installation management, run on Japanese business terms, so your overseas team is never blocked by local logistics.

04: Support

Work inside your own tools

Customer inquiries handled end-to-end (translated, ticketed in your support system, and followed up) so your product team sees clear, actionable communication in English.

The test Your customer experiences a responsive, Japanese-speaking partner. Your product team gets clear English. If either side ever has to say "something got lost in between," we've failed.

Engagement shape

30-day trial→ Monthly retainer→ Renewal on results

Engagements start with a low-risk 30-day trial and scale as a monthly retainer. The first project we ran this way converted from trial to ongoing retainer, and became the client's reference case for further business development in Japan.
