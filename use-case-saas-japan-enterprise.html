Cracking Japan's Enterprise Market: A SaaS Story

Japan contacts existed. Pipeline didn't. Dorado restructured the pitch, targeted the right buyers, and built 10 SQLs and 20 active deals in six months.

Note on naming An NDA is in place for this engagement. The client is referred to anonymously below. Before publishing with a real name, written approval from the client is required.

Client

A leading Southeast Asian real estate SaaS company targeting enterprise adoption in the Japanese market.

Glance

Within six months, Dorado generated 10 sales-qualified leads from well-known brands in the client's target vertical and expanded the total active pipeline to approximately 20 deal opportunities, each valued at ¥10M–¥20M.

Issue

The client had existing connections in Japan but was unable to convert them into meaningful pipeline. Outreach had consistently stalled at the operational level, failing to reach stakeholders with actual budget authority. Cultural and language barriers compounded the problem: the client's pitch and use cases had not been adapted for the Japanese enterprise context, and the channels being used were not reaching the right people at the right level.

Approach

Dorado applied a three-step process tailored to the realities of Japan's enterprise sales environment.

1. Understand the product

A deep-dive into the client's platform capabilities, competitive differentiation, and core value drivers as they applied to the Japanese real estate sector.

2. Define the ICP

Identification of precise decision-maker profiles, organizational buying dynamics, and priority target accounts within the vertical.

3. Build an offer that lands

Restructuring of the pitch, localization of use cases for the Japanese market, and selection of the right outreach channels to reach senior stakeholders rather than operational contacts.

10 SQLs, 20 active deals, ¥10–20M per deal. A substantial qualified pipeline from a market where the client had previously been unable to gain traction beyond initial contacts.

Engagement ROI

Over six months, Dorado delivered 10 sales-qualified leads, all from recognized enterprise brands within the target vertical. Expanding the view to include all accounts moved into active deal stages, the total pipeline reached approximately 20 opportunities. With per-deal values estimated at ¥10M–¥20M, the engagement created a substantial qualified pipeline from a market where the client had previously been unable to gain traction beyond initial contacts.
