Service 02

Proof before commitment.

Pilot exists for one moment: you believe Japan could work, but you're not ready to fund a full operation on belief. So the meter is built around evidence. For companies that want the market's answer before the bigger commitment. Deliberately narrow — sales only, by design.

Before you ask

The questions people
usually have about this page.

How do I choose between the three plans?

Q&A ($99/mo) if you mainly want to put your questions to someone who knows the market. Pilot ($150/mo + success fees) if you want a lighter, results-based way to prove sales traction first. Retainer ($1,500–6,000/mo) if you need the whole function — sales, customer success, product, research, strategy — run for you. Start where you are and move up as results earn it.

When should I go straight to the Retainer?

When your goal is to actually win and hold the market, not just test it — customers to keep, product to adapt, a strategy to set. Running those as five separate vendors is slower and more expensive than one integrated team. If Japan is a real priority, the Retainer is the plan built for it.

What counts as a "qualified" appointment on Pilot?

A held first meeting with a company matching the target criteria agreed at setup, attended by relevant stakeholders (procurement, technical, product, executive or business development), with a substantive conversation about your product, supply or partnership. No-shows, vendors selling to you, and generic information exchanges don't qualify. Exact criteria for your product are set before we start.

What makes a month "active" for a qualified lead?

Real deal progression: follow-up meetings, tailored proposals or quotes, technical and specification work, NDA / vendor registration / PoC coordination, expansion to additional stakeholders, or reactivating a dormant deal. Status checks, forwarding materials and unanswered outreach never qualify.

How do I know the success fees won't pile up unexpectedly?

Every billable meeting and lead is shared with you before it's invoiced. No-shows and quiet months bill $0, and nothing goes on an invoice you haven't already seen and confirmed.

Why is there a $150 fixed fee if Pilot is results-based?

Because a pipeline that isn't maintained dies quietly, and dead pipelines produce no results. The $150 keeps the buyer list current, replies tracked, and your monthly report delivered — the minimum for outcomes to keep coming between meetings.

Can I move from Pilot to the Retainer later?

Yes — that's the usual path. Everything built on Pilot (buyer list, pipeline, meeting records) carries straight into the Retainer, so nothing is re-done or re-explained.

What's the difference between Pilot and Retainer, really?

The outcome, not just the price. Pilot produces sales results — meetings and moving deals — and nothing else. The Retainer produces a running market operation: customer success, product management, research, marketing and strategy on top of sales.

Where can I see the exact qualifying criteria and a sample monthly report?

In a presentation, not on this page — the real criteria depend on your product and targets. Request one and we'll walk through your specific numbers, meeting by meeting and month by month.

How is the Retainer priced within $1,500–6,000?

By scope: how many functions you need running (sales alone vs. sales plus customer success, product, research, marketing, strategy) and how much of each. We size it exactly in the first call, not as a generic tier.
